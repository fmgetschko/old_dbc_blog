Stereotype Threat

April 03, 2015

I've made some research on stereotype threat. I've been giving a lot of thought lately on how impressive is the power that our mind has over us, even when we don't realize it. I think this subject is a clear example of it.

In life, we're constantly compared to others, and subsequentially we're usually judged after that. Luckily, I can't recall of any ocasions that I felt compared, and (mis)judged for being a woman. That obviously doesn't mean that it never happened, it just means that it didn't bother me then.

But I've probably felt affected by stereotype threat when it comes to social status. Here, down the tropics (specially in samba-land), people are constantly discriminated by their (lack of) economic possessions. Specially when you're a child or teenager, it's easy to feel uncapable of achieving something because you lack of means to do that. If you can't afford to have a private teacher, you won't be good at learning a second language; if you can't attend a sports club, you'll never be a good voleyball player; and so on. The good part of it is that you end up learning how to deal with it, and achieve your goals anyway.

Nowadays, I can't really think of sterotypes or situations that may make me feel uncapable of achieving my goals. I think my obstination surpasses whatever could put me in that kind of situation. I trully believe that we can go as far as we can handle. In other words: stereotype threat? Not here!
